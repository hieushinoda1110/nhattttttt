Matsuri is a Japanese festival and a way for local communities to gather and celebrate. Nearly all matsuri have some affiliation with Buddhism, Shintoism, four seasons or important historical events, though a few of them rely entirely on the beauty of the fireworks. At times, matsuri can have solemn religious parades taking the Shinto god from shrine to shrine. On other occasions, matsuri are common secular gatherings involving seasons.

Sushi is a traditional dish and is also a cultural feature of the country of the rising sun. Sushi is a Japanese dish consisting of rice mixed with vinegar combined with other ingredients. Since ancient times, the Japanese have known how to brew fish, shrimp and seafood into a handful of rice to keep their delicious taste.
